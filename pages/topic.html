Satellite Wi-Fi

Equity of access in remote communities

01

Unequitable Access

We live an unequal world, where access to essential technologies is not shared fairly across all communities. While many people can rely on reliable high-speed internet without questioning it, there are communities which aren't even given the opportunity.

MF research found that a 1% increase in the number of internet users in the sub-Saharan Africa led to an economic growth of 0.1-0.4% (as cited in García-Escribano, 2020). This growth with only a small increase in the number of internet users, demonstrates the importance of the internet for a community's economy. Another study found that found that 82% of households in sub-Saharan Africa do not have any internet access (Adotey, 2021). This not only affects the economy, but also many aspects of everyday life. One of the biggest being education, where internet access can influence students' academic performance. The European Journal of Educational Sciences did a study looking into the academic improvements of Senior School students in Cape Coast Metropolis, Ghana. Students with access to the internet “showed a higher improvement” in their academic's performance than those without (Amponsah et al., 2022). Illustrating the positive impact that access to online academic resources can have on students' performance. But there are communities where it would be too difficult or expensive to build infrastructure needed for typical internet use.

Remote communities often face higher costs due to difficult terrain, long distances between homes and a lack of existing infrastructure. These barriers make internet rollout unviable or too slow in more remote areas (Anritsu, 2024). Without having internet these communities are left at with a disadvantage in education, communication, and economic opportunities.

02

What can be done?

An emerging solution to these challenges is satellite-based internet connectivity. Satellite Wi-fi offers connection to the internet, where traditional infrastructure is limited or unavailable. Using an array of satellite orbiting the earth, communications can be sent to and from locations across the globe (NASA, 2026b). Making it a valuable technology for industries and people in rural or remote areas of the world. This is why we have decided to focus our research on Satellite Wi-Fi and its relation to the equity of access for underserved communities. Satellite wi-fi gives opportunities to underserved communities that may lack infrastructure. Providing benefits in education, economic growth, healthcare and disaster response.

Despite these advantages, satellite wi-fi also raises potential risks, around its potential misuse for warfare, privacy, and environmental impacts. These concerns highlight a need for us to look at the full picture and determine adopting this technology is the right decision. By investigating the opportunities, risks and choices that come with the widespread use of this technology, we can reach an informed conclusion.
